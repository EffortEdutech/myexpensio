myexpensio — user quick guide

Step-by-step reference for all core features. Select a topic below.

1
Accept your invitation
Your admin sends you an invite link. Open it, enter your email and set a password. You will be joined to your organisation automatically — no manual setup needed.
2
Log in
Go to the app and log in with your email and password. You will land on the Home screen with quick-action buttons.
3
Review your home screen
Home shows: Start Trip, Plan Trip, New Claim, and Export. Summary cards show trips this month, route calculations used, and claim counts.
4
Set your rates (optional)
Go to Settings → Rates to configure your mileage rate per KM (MYR), meal default, and lodging default. Your admin may have set org-wide defaults already.
New to the app? Start by recording a trip, then attach it to a claim, then submit. That is the full cycle.
GPS tracking (Free + Pro)
1
Tap Start Trip
Grant location permission when prompted. The app records GPS points in the background while you travel. You will see a "Tracking ON" indicator.
2
Tap Stop Trip when done
The trip is saved. You reach the Trip Summary screen showing start/end time and calculated distance.
3
Add odometer evidence (optional)
Upload photos of your odometer at start and end, or enter readings manually. Toggle "Use odometer as official distance" if you need to override GPS.
4
Save or Add to Claim
Tap Save Trip to keep it in your trip list, or Add to Claim to attach it directly to an open draft claim.
Route planning (Free: 2/month Free limit, Pro: unlimited Pro)
1
Tap Plan Trip
Enter origin and destination. Tap Get Routes. The app fetches route alternatives and counts as one route calculation against your monthly limit.
2
Select a route
Review alternatives (distance in KM, duration, summary). Tap Select This Route. Distance is locked as the official distance for reimbursement.
i
If you hit your Free route-calc limit, you can still record trips using GPS tracking — no route calculation is used.
1
Create a new claim
Tap New Claim from Home or the Claims tab. Set a title (e.g. "March Field Visits") and the claim period (start date → end date).
2
Add items
Tap Add Item and choose a type: Mileage, Meal, Lodging, Toll, Parking, or Bus. Fill in the details for each item. Attach receipts where required.
3
Attach a trip (mileage items)
For Mileage items, select a saved trip. The official distance (KM) and reimbursement amount are calculated automatically from your rate.
4
Review totals
The claim footer shows the total in MYR. Check all items before submitting.
5
Submit claim Locks on submit
Tap Submit Claim. Once submitted, the claim is locked — no edits are possible. Status changes to SUBMITTED and is ready for admin review.
A submitted claim cannot be edited. Double-check all items and amounts before tapping Submit.
1
Add a Toll item
In a claim, Add Item → Toll. Enter the amount paid (MYR) and choose payment method: Cash or TNG eWallet. Optionally import from a TNG statement.
2
Add a Parking item
Add Item → Parking. Enter amount, location (optional), and duration. Attach a receipt if available.
3
Add a Bus fare item
Add Item → Bus. Enter the fare amount. Select payment method (Cash or TNG). Enter origin and destination stops.
4
Import from TNG statement (Toll/Parking)
Upload your TNG eWallet PDF statement. The app parses transactions automatically and shows a picker — select the relevant transactions to import them as claim items.
i
TNG statement import works offline for previously uploaded statements. New uploads require a network connection.
1
Go to the Export tab
Tap Export from Home or the bottom navigation. You will see the export configuration screen.
2
Set date range and status
Choose a date range for claims. Status defaults to Submitted — this is the recommended setting for finance submission. You can also include Draft for internal reviews.
3
Choose format
Select CSV or XLSX. XLSX is recommended for finance teams as it preserves formatting and column structure.
4
Tap Generate Export
The file is generated and appears in the export history list. Tap Download to save it. Failed exports show a Retry option.
Export values are locked to submitted amounts — totals cannot change after submission, ensuring audit consistency.
1
Profile
Update your display name and company name (optional). These appear on exported claim reports.
2
Rates
Set your mileage rate per KM (MYR), meal default rate, and lodging default rate. Rates are applied when creating claim items. You can override per-item at claim time.
3
Location privacy
View your current location permission status. The app only accesses your location during an active trip — never in the background otherwise.
4
Sign out
Tap Sign Out at the bottom of Settings. Your data remains saved in your account.
