Why PDPA Applies to MyExpensio

Even though MyExpensio is invite-only, the moment we collect any of the following, Malaysian PDPA 2010 applies in full:

  • 📛 Name and email address of invitees (before they even sign up)
  • 📱 Phone number, profile information
  • 🗺️ Trip locations, routes, mileage logs
  • 📊 App usage analytics, device info, IP addresses
  • 💳 Touch 'n Go transaction data
⚠️ Invite-only does NOT exempt us. The law applies from the moment we store an invitee's email address — before they even click the link.

→

→
Step 4 ✅ Account Created Consent timestamp stored in DB
✅ All four steps are now covered by platform documents. See the Platform Legal Documents section below.

What must happen at each step

  • 📧Invite email: Must include PDPA legal footer, inviter name, and a path to request email deletion if received in error.
  • 🔗Accept invite page: Must show a short pre-consent notice explaining we received their email from the inviter. This is the PDPA-critical moment before data collection escalates.
  • ☐Signup form: Must show two unchecked checkboxes (required terms + optional marketing). Must link to both legal documents. Age confirmation required.
  • 🗄️Account created: Backend must log consent_terms: true, consent_marketing: bool, consented_at: timestamp, consented_ip: string.
4

The Two Data Moments in Invite-Only Signup

Unlike regular signups, invite-only platforms have two distinct personal data events, and PDPA applies to both.

📩 Moment 1 — Invite is sent

The invitee's email is stored before they consent to anything. This is allowed, but we must:

  • Include a legal footer in the email
  • Commit to deleting the email within 14 days if not accepted
  • Show the inviter a disclosure when they send the invite

📝 Moment 2 — Account is created

The user provides full personal data. At this point we must:

  • Show the PDPA pre-consent notice
  • Capture explicit, affirmative consent via checkbox
  • Log timestamp and IP of consent
⚠️ Key PDPA wording to include in Privacy Policy: "We may receive your email address from an existing user who invited you to join the platform." This must be explicitly stated.
5

Consent Rules (PDPA Critical)

  • ☐ Checkboxes must not be pre-checked. User must actively opt in.
  • 🔗 Checkbox labels must link to the live Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.
  • 🔀 Terms consent and marketing consent must be separate checkboxes — never bundled.
  • 🔒 The Create Account button must remain disabled until the required checkbox is checked.
  • 🗄️ Consent must be logged in the database with a timestamp and IP address.
  • ↩️ Users must be able to withdraw consent at any time by emailing support@myexpensio.com.
💬 Required checkbox wording (exact):

"I have read and agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, and I consent to the collection, use and disclosure of my personal data in accordance with the Privacy Policy."
6

Location & Mileage Data — Special Notice

Because MyExpensio is a mileage app, location data is collected. This is treated as medium-sensitivity data and must be clearly disclosed in the Privacy Policy.

  • 📍 Privacy Policy must state when location is collected (only during trip tracking).
  • 🎯 Must state why — to calculate travel distance for mileage and reimbursement.
  • 💾 Must clarify whether location is stored or only processed in real time.
  • 🔕 Must clarify that background tracking is not used (if applicable).
✅ Required Privacy Policy statement:
"We collect location data only when you use the trip tracking feature to calculate travel distance. Location data is not collected in the background."
⚠️ Mileage accuracy disclaimer — must appear in both Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. Calculations are for convenience only and should not be used for tax, legal, or employer reimbursement purposes without independent verification.
7

User Rights Under PDPA

Users have four core rights under Malaysian PDPA Part III. We must provide a path for all of them.

RightWhat it meansHow we handle it (MVP)
Access User can ask what data we hold on them Email support@myexpensio.com
Correction User can ask us to fix inaccurate data In-app profile edit + email
Withdraw Consent User can revoke consent to processing Email support@myexpensio.com
Deletion User can request account + data deletion In-app delete account button + email
📬 All data rights requests must be acknowledged within a reasonable period. For MVP, routing to support@myexpensio.com is acceptable.
8

Invite Sender Disclosure

When an admin or user sends an invite, they are sharing a third party's email with us. PDPA requires that the sender confirms they have permission to do so.

💬 Show this notice on the invite screen:

"By sending an invitation, you confirm you have permission to share this person's email address with MyExpensio."

This one line protects us from complaints like "Why does this app have my email?" and demonstrates due diligence under PDPA.
